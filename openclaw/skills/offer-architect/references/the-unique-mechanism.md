## What Is A Mechanism

Every product or service has a mechanism which is used to deliver the results.

The mechanism is how the product or service delivers the promised results/outcome.

There is two types of mechanisms:

A Commoditized Mechanism

A Unique Mechanism

## Commoditized Mechanism

The moment you introduce a commoditized mechanism you open yourself up to comparison to every other service that uses that mechanism.

Let's say Company A spends time educating a consumer on the benefits of regularly servicing their furnace because that is the service they are offering.

As soon as they have brought awareness to their prospects need to regularly service their furnace they jump over to

Google or Amazon or any number of places and start price shopping, looking for options, etc.

Maybe theres a book on that, or a YouTube video, or someone cheaper

There is no reason to do business with that company.

You NEVER EVER want to introduce a commoditized mechanism.

## Unique Mechanism

This is the distinctive system, steps, process, framework, or method that underpins your product or service, creating the results your customers seek.

It explains how and why your offering can deliver on its promises, setting it apart from others.

This mechanism is proprietary, innovative, and available exclusively through your business.

Compare the last example to your company and your Unique Mechanism

Lets say you offer a patented diagnostic systems that provides a comprehensive health report of the furnace in under 15 minutes.

The tool predicts potential failures before they happen, ensuring uninterrupted heating during winter.

If your prospects now go to search Google, Amazon, or wherever else, the only results lead back to you.

Products/Services and UM's are not your offer.

Your Unique Mechanism will often stay the same regardless of how you deliver your offers.

## The Difference

Do you see how one example educates the market on the benefits of regularly servicing their furnace and creates demand for that service

while the other educates them on the benefits of doing business with you and creates demand to do business with you.

Demand for the service vs. Demand for YOUR SERVICE!

## USP's vs. UM's

A USP is when your product or service delivers a unique benefit whereas a UM is the way in which you deliver that benefit.

The reality is is that there are very few USP's anymore unless we are talking about a new and truly innovative solution.

Netflix vs movie rentals

Uber vs taxi's

That is ok.

That is all the more reason you need a UM to differentiate yourself.

## The Value Of Creating A UM


## Why Create A UM?

Differentiation is CRITICAL!

Nobody seeks out the common.

Being different is better than being better when it comes to getting attention.

(being different gets attention but being able to prove and explain why it is superior gets the conversion)

Differentiating solely on the length of time you have been in business, your experience, your credentials, your track record, etc. is no longer effective

Having a UM gives you the ability to differentiate with something new.

Unique Mechanisms (UM) give you the ability to differentiate while promising the same outcome as competitors.

UM answers the question "Why this?" for your prospects.

UM gives your prospects hope by showcasing a unique approach.

While it may give hope you must explain why it gets the results& you can't just say it, prove it.

UM enables you to market effectively to more sophisticated and discerning markets.

UM allows you to use Education-Based Marketing (EBM) to inform and attract potential customers.

A unique mechanism is NOT your product, it is a component of your product.

Your product/service is how your prospects experience, benefit, gains access to your UM

When you are educating you are not educating on your product/service you are educating on the unique mechanism.

## Levels Of Market Sophistication

![Levels Of Market Sophistication]

Sticking with the HVAC example:
### Level 1: Basic Claim
> Example: "We provide HVAC repair services."
### Level 2: Amplified Claim
> Example: "We provide fast and reliable HVAC repair services in 24 hours."
### Level 3: Unique Mechanism
> Example: "We use the RapidCheck Diagnostic System to identify issues quickly and accurately."
### Level 4: Improved Mechanism
> Example: "Our HVAC services not only use RapidCheck Diagnostics but also include a comprehensive maintenance plan to prevent future issues."
### Level 5: Market Saturation and Sophistication
> Example: "Join thousands of satisfied customers who trust our AI-driven RapidCheck Diagnostics and preventive maintenance plans for 99% uptime in HVAC performance."
The chances are that you will continue to operate in level 3 & 4.

You can continually create new and better mechanisms.

Ultimately in a crowded market, what the prospect really wants is the outcome, it's your job to uncover why your way of delivering that outcome is a superior

and different way to deliver that outcome and to put that into a proprietary framework or unique mechanism

## Types of Unique Mechanisms

- Existing
- Unspoken
- Transubstantiated
- Metaphorical

## Existing Mechanisms

These are mechanisms that already exist in the market but can still be highlighted to differentiate your offering.

Usually, through your unique way of delivering.

While the mechanism itself is not new or unique, the way it is presented, utilized, or integrated into your product or service can make it stand out. By focusing on an existing mechanism, you can leverage its proven reliability and

effectiveness to build trust and demonstrate value to your customers.

## Unspoken Mechanisms

An unspoken mechanism relates to the fact that the way of delivering the product or service is unknown to the market.

This does not mean that the mechanism itself is unique.

It simply means that the way in which the product or service offers the benefit is unique to the market.

A example is the one seen above

For example

At the time no one in the beer industry was talking about how beer bottles were cleaned and sterilized before production even though they all were.

Claude Hopkins realized this and created a unspoken mechanism that helped drastically boost the sales of Schlitz Beer

## Transubstantiated Mechanisms

Taking the ordinary and transforming it into something extraordinary.

Basic HVAC Filter Replacement → PureFlow Filtration System

Basic Energy Efficiency → SmartEnergy Optimization

## The Metaphorical Mechanism

This is what you would most likely use when you are selling a pure commodity.

A metaphorical mechanism uses a metaphor or analogy to present a common or commoditized product in a unique and compelling way.

This technique reframes the product or service, making it stand out by creating a more engaging and memorable concept for the customer.

Installing energy-efficient HVAC units to reduce energy consumption. → Experience the "Green Shield" Installation that not only cools your home but also shields it from high energy bills and environmental impact."

You're essentially hiding the common behind a nickname.

To come up with a metaphorical mechanism ask yourself what is this like?

## Looking At Competitors

- What is the mechanism behind their offer?
- How does their method/mechanism work?
- What is positive about their approach?
- What is negative about their approach?
- Are they solving a root cause or a symptom of the root cause.

## Your Mechanism

Questions to ask yourself (they may not all apply)
- What is my approach?
- How does my approach work?
- Why does my approach work? (symptom vs root cause?)
- What is the reason I approach it this way?
- Why do I do it in that way (why does the order matter)
- What have I added or subtracted to an existing offerings (mine or others)
- Is there anything that I have combined? Why?
- Why is mine easier/faster/cheaper/more reliable/more consistent/safer
Note: If you can create something that is easier or faster that is highly desirable in most markets
- Why does my way produce better results?

## Naming Your Unique Mechanism

Clarity on how and why your process works**.

The Name

Creating a UM is not slapping a fancy name on a generic process.

**understanding how and why your process works is far more important.

You do not want your name to sound salesy, overhyped. and benefit driven You want it to sound credible, legit and scientific

Save the overhyped names for your products and service offers.

Examples of overhyped: No

## 2 Types Of Unique Mechanisms

1. Root Cause
- What is the real root cause of the problem the prospect is facing?
- What is the symptom(s) competitors are addressing with their mechanisms?
- What evidence/support can you share to show the prospect the real root cause of their problem?
- How does your Unique Mechanism solve the root cause?
- What are the benefits of having the real root cause solved?
2. The Better (Symptoms)
- What does <better" mean? (i.e. Is it easier, faster, more effective, more consistent, cheaper)
- Why is it <better" (i.e. How does it work)
- What evidence/support can you share to show why your Unique Mechanism is <better"?
- What are the <better" benefits of your Unique Mechanism?
