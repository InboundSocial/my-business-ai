# Copywriting Formulas

Reference doc for proven copywriting formulas. Load when you need specific structures.

---

## Headline Formulas

### The "How To" Formula
**Structure:** How to [desirable outcome] without [pain/sacrifice]

**Examples:**
- How to Add $47K/Month Without Hiring a Single Employee
- How to Get 10 Hours/Week Back Without Giving Up Control
- How to Book More Jobs Without Spending More on Ads

**Why it works:** Promises a specific outcome while removing an objection upfront.

---

### The "Secret" Formula
**Structure:** The [adjective] secret to [outcome] that [authority] uses

**Examples:**
- The Hiring Secret $50M Franchises Use (That You've Never Heard Of)
- The Strange Reason Your Best Tech Keeps Quitting
- The One System Every 7-Figure Contractor Has (And You Don't)

**Why it works:** Creates curiosity gap + borrows authority from a known entity.

---

### The "Number + Timeframe" Formula
**Structure:** [Number] ways/steps/secrets to [outcome] in [timeframe]

**Examples:**
- 4 Systems That Cut Owner Hours in Half in 60 Days
- The 3-Step Process That Books Jobs While You Sleep
- 5 SOPs Every Painting Company Needs (That Take 30 Minutes to Implement)

**Why it works:** Specificity + timeframe makes it concrete and believable.

---

### The "What If" Formula
**Structure:** What if you could [dream outcome] without [biggest objection]?

**Examples:**
- What If You Could Take Friday Off Without Your Phone Blowing Up?
- What If Your Team Actually Followed the Process — Every Time?
- What If New Leads Got Called Back in 5 Minutes, Not 5 Hours?

**Why it works:** Paints the "after" state while addressing skepticism.

---

### The "Problem-Agitation" Formula
**Structure:** [Problem question]. [Agitation]. [Here's why + hint at solution]

**Examples:**
- Still working 70 hours a week? You didn't start a business to become its prisoner. 
  There's a reason franchises work without their owners. You can have that too.

**Why it works:** Meets them where they are, makes pain vivid, offers hope.

## Lead Formulas

### The Story Lead (Unaware Audience)

```
When [person similar to reader] first [tried/started/faced X], they had no idea 
[surprising outcome] was even possible.

[Brief story of transformation]

Today, [current enviable state].

The difference? [Mechanism hint].
```

**Example:**
> When Mike started his HVAC company, he figured 60-hour weeks were just 
> part of the deal. No one told him it was possible to run a $2M business 
> and still make his kid's baseball games.
> 
> Last Tuesday, he left at 3pm. His phone didn't ring once.
> 
> The difference? He stopped running a business and started running systems.

---

### The Problem Lead (Problem-Aware Audience)

```
If you're [struggling with specific problem], you're not alone.

[Statistic or common experience].

The frustrating part? [Why what they've tried hasn't worked].

That's because [root cause reveal].
```

**Example:**
> If you're working 70+ hours a week and your business still feels fragile, 
> you're not alone.
> 
> 73% of home service owners report working more hours than when they had 
> a job — with less security.
> 
> The frustrating part? You've probably tried courses, coaches, maybe even 
> hired people who disappointed you.
> 
> That's because knowledge isn't the problem. Infrastructure is.

---

### The Mechanism Lead (Solution-Aware Audience)

```
There's a new approach to [problem] that [differentiator].

It's called [Mechanism Name].

Unlike [old approach], this [key difference].

Here's how it works:
```

**Example:**
> There's a new approach to getting off the truck that doesn't require you 
> to implement anything yourself.
> 
> It's called The Operating System Installation.
> 
> Unlike coaching (where YOU do the work) or franchises (where you pay 
> forever), this is done-for-you system building with a one-time investment.
> 
> Here's how it works:

---

### The Offer Lead (Most Aware Audience)

```
Get [specific outcome] in [timeframe] — guaranteed.

Here's exactly what you get:
- [Deliverable 1]
- [Deliverable 2]
- [Deliverable 3]

[Risk reversal].

[CTA].
```

**Example:**
> Get 4 franchise-level systems installed in your business in 60 days — 
> or pay nothing.
> 
> Here's exactly what you get:
> - Complete operating playbooks for your trade
> - Team training with live implementation
> - 60-day done-for-you build
> - The Owner Freedom Dashboard
> 
> If 3 systems aren't running by Day 45, you get a full refund.
> 
> Book your installation call.

## Body Copy Formulas

### The PAS Formula (Problem-Agitation-Solution)

```
[Problem — state the problem clearly]

[Agitation — make the pain vivid, show consequences]

[Solution — introduce your mechanism as the answer]
```

**Best for:** Middle-of-page sections, email copy, short-form content.

---

### The BAB Formula (Before-After-Bridge)

```
[Before — their current painful state]

[After — the dream outcome, painted vividly]

[Bridge — your mechanism is the bridge between them]
```

**Best for:** Hero sections, Facebook ads, quick pitches.

---

### The AIDA Formula (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

```
[Attention — headline/hook that stops them]

[Interest — expand on the promise, add curiosity]

[Desire — stack benefits, paint the "after," build proof]

[Action — clear CTA with urgency]
```

**Best for:** Full landing pages, sales letters, email sequences.

---

### The "Paradigm Shift" Formula (Schwartz-style)

```
You know how most [people like reader] think [common belief]?

And because of that, they [common action].

Which ultimately leads to [bad consequence].

So what we do instead is [different approach].

Which means [good outcome].
```

**Example:**
> You know how most contractors think the only way to grow is to 
> hire more people?
> 
> And because of that, they end up with more headaches, more HR drama, 
> and thinner margins.
> 
> Which ultimately leads to a bigger business that's somehow harder to run.
> 
> So what we do instead is install SYSTEMS before scaling headcount.
> 
> Which means you can double revenue without doubling your team — 
> or your stress.

## CTA Formulas

### The "Next Step" CTA
```
[Action verb] + [what they get] + [what happens next]
```

- "Book your installation call. You'll see exactly what we'd build, no pressure."
- "Get the playbook. It's free. Arrive in your inbox in 2 minutes."

### The "Urgency" CTA (only if real)
```
[Action] + [scarcity/deadline] + [consequence of waiting]
```

- "Only 5 installation spots this month. After that, you're on a 6-week waitlist."
- "Price increases January 1st. Lock in current pricing now."

### The "Risk Reversal" CTA
```
[Action] + [guarantee reminder]
```

- "Book your call. If we can't help, we'll tell you upfront — and recommend someone who can."
- "Get started. Remember: 45 days, 3 systems running, or you pay nothing."

## Quick Reference: Which Formula When?

| Situation | Formula |
|-----------|---------|
| Cold audience, need to hook | Story Lead |
| Audience knows the problem | Problem Lead → PAS |
| Audience knows solutions exist | Mechanism Lead |
| Warm audience, ready to buy | Offer Lead → Stack |
| Mid-page section | Paradigm Shift |
| Email copy | BAB or PAS |
| Full sales page | AIDA structure |
