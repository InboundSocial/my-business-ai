# Copywriting Anti-Patterns

What NOT to do. This is often the most valuable reference — it tells you what to avoid
so Claude's defaults don't leak through.

---

## AI Slop Phrases (Never Use)

These phrases are instant tells that a robot wrote the copy. They're generic,
overused, and fail to persuade.

### Hype Words

| Phrase | Why It Fails |
|--------|--------------|
| "Revolutionary" | Empty hype. What does it actually change? |
| "Game-changing" | Meaningless without specifics |
| "Cutting-edge" | Vague tech-speak |
| "World-class" | Unverifiable claim |
| "Best-in-class" | Same |
| "Premium" | Describes price, not value |
| "Seamless" | Usually untrue, always vague |
| "Comprehensive" | If everything is included, nothing is special |
| "Robust" | Engineer speak, means nothing to buyers |
| "Leverage" | Corporate jargon |
| "Synergy" | 1990s called |
| "Optimize" | What, specifically? |

### Dead Openers

| Phrase | Why It Fails |
|--------|--------------|
| "In today's fast-paced world..." | Dated, generic, everyone uses it |
| "Are you tired of...?" | Weak start, yes/no question |
| "Discover how..." | Passive, delays the promise |
| "Unlock the power of..." | Meaningless hype |
| "Introducing..." | Who cares about introductions? |
| "Welcome to..." | They're not here for hospitality |
| "We're excited to announce..." | Makes it about you, not them |
| "Imagine if..." | Weak. Just tell them what they get. |
| "What if I told you..." | Gimmicky |

### Feature-Speak

| Phrase | Why It Fails | Rewrite |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| "Our platform enables..." | Feature-first, not outcome-first | "Get [outcome]..." |
| "Designed to help you..." | Passive, indirect | "This gives you..." |
| "Built with [feature]..." | Nobody cares about features | "[Feature] means you can..." |
| "Powered by AI..." | Tech flex, not benefit | "AI does X so you don't have to" |
| "Easy-to-use interface" | Everyone claims this | "Takes 5 minutes to learn" |
| "All-in-one solution" | Vague, unbelievable | "Handles X, Y, and Z from one dashboard" |

### Weak Verbs

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| Learn how to | Get |
| Discover | See |
| Explore | Use |
| Understand | Know |
| Experience | Feel |
| Benefit from | Get |
| Leverage | Use |
| Utilize | Use |

## Structural Mistakes

### 1. Feature-First Copy

**Bad:**
> Our platform features AI-powered automation, real-time analytics, 
> and seamless integrations with your existing tools.

**Good:**
> Wake up to a full calendar. No calls. No chasing. No wondering where 
> the next job is coming from.

**Why it fails:** Nobody buys features. They buy outcomes. Features are 
proof that outcomes are achievable, but they come AFTER the outcome promise.

---

### 2. Multiple CTAs

**Bad:**
> Ready to get started? Schedule a call, or download our free guide, 
> or join our upcoming webinar to learn more.

**Good:**
> Book your installation call. We'll show you exactly what we'd build.

**Why it fails:** Confused prospects don't act. Give them ONE action.
The moment they have to decide between options, friction increases.

---

### 3. Talking About Yourself

**Bad:**
> At 1000x Sales, we've spent over a decade perfecting our methodology.
> Our team of certified experts brings deep expertise in...

**Good:**
> Imagine checking your phone at 2pm on a Tuesday and seeing your schedule 
> is full through next month — and you didn't lift a finger.

**Why it fails:** They don't care about you. They care about themselves.
Every sentence should be about THEIR outcome, THEIR life, THEIR problem.

---

### 4. Vague Promises

**Bad:**
> Transform your business and achieve operational excellence.

**Good:**
> Cut your work week from 70 hours to 40. Keep your income the same.

**Why it fails:** If your promise could appear on any competitor's page, 
it describes nothing. Specificity = believability.

---

### 5. No Proof

**Bad:**
> Our system dramatically increases efficiency and reduces costs.

**Good:**
> Last month, a 6-person painting crew using our playbooks completed 
> 23% more jobs with zero overtime. Here's the breakdown...

**Why it fails:** Claims without evidence are just noise. Every claim 
needs proof: numbers, testimonials, case studies, logic, or demonstrations.

---

### 6. Feature-as-Hook (The Silent Killer)

**Bad:**
> I built an AI that reads your pricing and answers customer questions.
> An AI trained on your business data that responds like your best employee.
> Your phone answered with his exact price, his warranty, and his service area.

**Good:**
> His new guy quoted the exact right price on his first solo call.
> The customer asked how long he'd been doing this. "It's my first week."

**Why it fails:** Describing what a product DOES is not a hook. It's a feature
list wearing a costume. The reader nods, understands, and scrolls. There's no
question to answer, no loop to close, no story to follow.

The fix: describe a SITUATION that happened (or could happen) because of the
product. Never mention the product. Never describe the feature. Put a PERSON
in a SCENARIO with a REACTION. The reader should want to know what happened,
not what the product does.

**The test:** Can you delete every mention of the product/technology and the
hook still works? If not, you wrote a product description, not a hook.

---

### 7. Burying the Lead

**Bad:**
> In the ever-evolving landscape of home services, business owners face 
> increasing challenges. From hiring to marketing to operations, the 
> complexity of running a successful company has never been higher. 
> That's why we created a solution that addresses these challenges head-on...

**Good:**
> You didn't start a business to work 70 hours a week.

**Why it fails:** They're deciding in 8 seconds. Don't waste the first 
100 words on throat-clearing. Hit them immediately.

---

### 8. Passive Voice

**Bad:**
> Your business will be transformed by our proven methodology.

**Good:**
> Our methodology transforms your business.

**Better:**
> We transform your business.

**Best:**
> You get a transformed business.

**Why it fails:** Passive voice is weak, indirect, and harder to read.
Active voice is confident and clear.

---

### 9. Being Vague to Sound Sophisticated

**Bad:**
> Enhance your operational paradigm through systematic process optimization.

**Good:**
> Stop doing the same tasks twice. Here's how.

**Why it fails:** Jargon doesn't impress — it confuses. Simple language 
is more persuasive because it's more trustworthy.

## Tone Anti-Patterns

### Too Salesy

**Signs:**
- Exclamation points everywhere!!!
- CAPS for emphasis
- "But wait, there's more!"
- Countdown timers with fake urgency
- "ACT NOW!!!"

**Why it fails:** Screams desperation. Sophisticated buyers run.

---

### Too Casual / "Bro"

**Signs:**
- "Yo, what's up?"
- "This is gonna blow your mind"
- "Dude, seriously..."
- Excessive slang

**Why it fails:** Unprofessional. Works for some markets, kills credibility in B2B.

---

### Too Corporate

**Signs:**
- "We are pleased to offer..."
- "Upon completion of the onboarding process..."
- "Kindly note that..."
- Third-person references to the company

**Why it fails:** Sounds like a legal document. Nobody feels connected to a corporation.

---

### Too Humble

**Signs:**
- "We humbly believe..."
- "We just want to help..."
- "We're not perfect, but..."
- Apologizing for the offer

**Why it fails:** Lacks confidence. If you don't believe in what you're selling, 
why should they?

---

## The Meta-Check

After writing, ask yourself:

1. **Would I keep reading this?**
   If you're bored, they're already gone.

2. **Could this describe any competitor?**
   If yes, it's too generic. Find the specificity.

3. **Is there a single sentence I'd highlight?**
   If not, nothing is memorable.

4. **Did I prove anything, or just claim it?**
   Claims without proof are noise.

5. **Is it clear what to do next?**
   Confusion kills conversions.

6. **Does it sound like marketing, or like a smart friend?**
   Rewrite until it sounds human.
