# Subsystem Decomposition Examples

Every job type should be broken into **subsystems**, then into **line items** with unit costs. This is the framework that makes pricing accurate and defensible.

## Why Subsystem Decomposition

Most contractors price jobs as a single lump number. That works until:
- A job takes longer than expected and you don't know which part went over
- You need to train a new tech and can't explain what's included
- A customer asks "why does this cost that much?" and you can't break it down
- You want to offer Good/Better/Best options and don't know where the cost splits

Subsystem decomposition solves all of this. You price the pieces, and the total builds itself.

## The Framework

### Step 1: Identify Subsystems

Every job has 2-5 subsystems. Think of them as the "systems" or "areas" of the job.

### Step 2: List Line Items per Subsystem

Each line item should be small enough that:
- It happens the same way most times
- It has consistent time + materials
- It can be added/removed without rewriting the whole estimate

### Step 3: Assign Units and Costs

Each line item gets:
- **System tag**: Which subsystem it belongs to
- **Unit**: each / per foot / per fitting / per hour
- **Labor**: Minutes by role (Journeyman, Apprentice, Helper)
- **Materials**: Short bill of materials or a "kit" (bundled items)
- **Exclusions**: What's included, what triggers an adder

## Example: Water Heater Install (Plumbing)

### Subsystems
1. **Water System** — supply lines to/from tank
2. **Gas System** — gas line connection (if gas unit)
3. **Venting System** — exhaust venting (if combustion unit)
4. **General** — removal, setup, cleanup, permits

### Line Items

**Water System**
| Line Item | Unit | Qty | Material | Labor | Role |
|-----------|------|-----|----------|-------|------|
| Disconnect/reconnect water supply | each | 1 | $15 (flex lines, fittings) | 30 min | Journeyman |
| Water supply valve replace (if needed) | each | 0-2 | $25/ea (ball valve) | 20 min/ea | Journeyman |
| T&P relief valve + drain line | each | 1 | $20 (valve + tube) | 15 min | Journeyman |

**Gas System**
| Line Item | Unit | Qty | Material | Labor | Role |
|-----------|------|-----|----------|-------|------|
| Gas flex connector | each | 1 | $30 (connector + fittings) | 20 min | Journeyman |
| Gas shutoff valve (if needed) | each | 0-1 | $35 | 25 min | Journeyman |
| Gas leak test | each | 1 | $5 (soap/detector) | 10 min | Journeyman |

**Venting System**
| Line Item | Unit | Qty | Material | Labor | Role |
|-----------|------|-----|----------|-------|------|
| Standard B-vent reconnect | each | 1 | $40 (connectors, screws) | 30 min | Journeyman |
| Vent pipe extension (if needed) | per ft | 0-6 | $8/ft | 10 min/ft | Journeyman |
| Power vent conversion (if upgrading) | each | 0-1 | $200+ (kit) | 60 min | Journeyman |

**General**
| Line Item | Unit | Qty | Material | Labor | Role |
|-----------|------|-----|----------|-------|------|
| Old unit removal + haul | each | 1 | — | 30 min | Apprentice |
| New unit positioning | each | 1 | — | 20 min | Journeyman + Apprentice |
| Drain pan (if required) | each | 0-1 | $25 | 15 min | Apprentice |
| Code compliance check | each | 1 | — | 15 min | Journeyman |
| Cleanup + customer walkthrough | each | 1 | — | 15 min | Journeyman |
| Permit pull (if required) | each | 0-1 | $75-150 | 30 min | Office |

### Adders (What Triggers an Upcharge)
- Unit is in a tight crawlspace or attic: +$150-300 (extra time + difficulty)
- Code requires expansion tank: +$75-125 (tank + install)
- Earthquake strapping required: +$50-75
- Long gas line run (>6 ft): +$15/ft
- Old unit has asbestos insulation: +$200+ (abatement)
- Permit required: +permit cost + $50 coordination
